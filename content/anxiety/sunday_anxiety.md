Title: Sunday anxiety & coping strategies
Date: 2019-10-06
Category: anxiety

As today is Sunday, I will talk about anxiety because since when I remember 
this is my “official day” for anxiety to grow. I always felt bad on Sundays 
because the next day would be Monday. My problem with Mondays is the unexpected 
environment waiting for me in which I will need to deal with people, using a 
lot of energy to that end.

Because I was always like that, I have developed some strategies to cope 
with this anxiety, otherwise, I would not leave home and function in my 
own life. I listed them below.

1. **Planning the week ahead**. A huge part of my anxiety comes from the unexpected 
day/week ahead, so, for me, one of the most effective ways to cope with my anxiety
 is planning my week ahead. I reached a point in my life that I realised I NEED 
 to know beforehand what I am going to do in the week to be functional. 
 If I don’t plan my week before it starts, my level of anxiety is enormous 
 and I will probably find an excuse to stay at home or even physically manifest 
 some kind of bad thing to actually need to stay at home, not being able to even 
 leave my bed. I tried a lot to resist my need to know things earlier, it didn’t 
 take me anywhere. So I just decided to accept it: I am like this, I need 
 planning in my life and that’s okay. In Brazil we have this saying “just 
 accept it because it hurts less”. That’s exactly what I did, I accepted and 
 started to consistently plan my week ahead. I am happier now. 
 2. **Following my weekdays' routine during the weekend**. I am a person who 
 loves routine. I absolutely love do the same thing day after day after day…
 I just love that my days have the same structure, I need structure in my life. 
 So another thing that troubled me during the weekend was the difference in 
 the structure of the days. I usually struggle a lot with changes, so when 
 it was Sunday, I started to suffer in anticipation because the next day would
  be different from the last one. I know it sounds weird, but that’s how my 
  brain works, it doesn’t like things differently. So, what I started to do? 
  To keep on my weekends the exactly the same structure from my weekdays and 
  it does wonders do help me with my anxiety: I don’t need to suffer over my 
  days being different anymore. I keep the same sleeping and waking time, 
  my exercises hours, my blocks of deep work, and several other things 
  (I will soon write a post about my current routine). I am aware that most of 
  the people would find this tough to follow, but for me, it is necessary and 
  actually makes me happier and calmer. 
  3. **Enjoying the day**. This one seems obvious but if you are anxious, you will 
  find hard to enjoy the present moment. I used to be like this, I would spend 
  my whole Sunday suffering over the next day and I would just miss my day. 
  So nowadays I consciously set an intention to enjoy my day. I enjoy that 
  usually I don’t need to be around people on Sundays, apart from my husband 
  but I love being in his presence, I enjoy having more time to read, to walk 
  outside, to cook, to clean my house, to study things I love... I just enjoy 
  the whole day, without thinking about the next day, it is already planned 
  on my calendar and I know exactly how it will look, so I can take it off my mind. 
  
  I have other coping mechanisms for anxiety, but they are daily activities 
  such as meditation, exercises, outdoor time, journaling... I can talk about them in another post. 
  
  Because I have accepted how my brain works and its needs, I can work on actions 
  to help me functioning and do what I want for my life without being held back by my anxiety :)
  
  Have a great Sunday.
