I believe that people’s needs for social interactions have different levels. 
I know that I have way less need to be social or to be with others than most people around me. 
People usually want to be around others during mealtime or just socialise and talk anything, whereas I prefer to spend most of my time alone. I enjoy having meals alone or with the company of a book, a piece of music or just my own thoughts. I also like the quiet company of someone I love, for example, my husband. I love to sit beside him during meals and not necessary talking, but just enjoying the silence. 
I love silence. I appreciate just to be with someone, without being involved in an interaction, that’s not a problem for me. 

It is not that I never enjoy socialising with people, I do, but only within a small part of my time. When I was in college, I used to joke that I had a daily quota of social interactions, and when I reach this quota, I cannot interact anymore. I used to say that without realising how much truth was that. Today I understand that it is related to energy. I don’t have enough energy to be with people all the time. Interactions in ***ANY*** way demands a lot of energy from myself, and if I require too much from my energy resource, I will be drained and non-functional. I am learning how to manage my energy better now (maybe a subject to another post). 

When I first came to Ireland to my internship, I came alone. I remember that I would go to work and initially all the time off work I had I would spend alone. It was like heaven! The funny part is that a lot of people back in Brazil (and even people from work) were worried that I would be depressed or too alone if I didn’t go out with other people. But it was absolutely fine! I was happy, good, in peace. It was perfect for me. During the week I would use my social energy to work with people, so during the weekend, I found pleasure and peace by being alone. I remember spending my weekend days on long walks, on Cafes reading or writing, I wrote a lot of letters for people I love. After some time I came to know people better and started to accept invitations to do something together. It was fun too, I had an amazing time with people. But, as I said, for me time with people must be in small and sparse doses. 

That's why I feel that having lunch with people all weekdays at work is too much for me. I feel so tired after interacting with people during lunch, I much more prefer to have my lunches alone or just with my husband. I am thinking about how to deal with this. Yet I haven’t found a solution. Don’t get me wrong, people from work are really nice and friendly and I like them. It is just too much for me, just that. Nothing personal. Especially when, how it is these days, there are several people together during lunch, all talking and making sounds… it is so demanding to deal with. One solution is to change my mealtime, but like I said before I am a creature of routine, I don’t like the idea of changing my mealtime to later or earlier. But anyway, I will next week try some strategies on how to hide from people during my lunchtime.

I am happy to talk about this, for me, it is a step more to self-acceptance. For too long I have dealt with myself thinking that I was wrong for being different. I am not, I am just the way I am, not better, not worse, just myself. It is good to embrace who we are. : )
