Hello!
I can't believe I am finally back on blogging! I've been wanting to come back for some time now, I am happy I found the time to return. 
For the first post, I will publish a reviewed version of an old post about my "touch sensitivities". In general, I don't like when people touch me, but I especially dislike when they do this unexpectedly. 
For example when someone tries to catch my attention by touching me. I hate it! 

To me, these touches are like fire or electricity on my skin and this sensation remains for quite sometime after the touch. 
It makes me uneasy and for some time, I can only think about this sensation and how it troubles me. 
When someone touches me, my brain stops, it seems as if I am underwater or I am watching the situation from outside my body. 
I can't react properly, I can't process anymore...I am basically there waiting to return to myself at some point. 
Usually, I look to the person without reaction. I wish I could react like crazy yelling "don't touch me!!!" like those funny gifs, but I just can't. Mostly because my brain stops and when I return to myself the moment already passed. 

I can't handle very well with hugs too.  I do like hugs, but I kind of have a limit. 
If you hug me for too long, it's awful for me. I feel as I'm suffocating, it seems I just can't breathe. 
It happens even with people who I absolutely love and are close to me. Even with my siblings and my parents. 
My youngest sister and bother love to hug me and I frequently recoil myself under their hugs. 

One beloved friend of mine used to like to hug me too long and this would make me feel awkward. 
After his hug, I would spend a lot of time trying to come back to normal because it really made me feel uneasy.  
I couldn't say it to him because I know he hugged me like that because he was demonstrating that I'm a special friend to him, I didn't want to hurt his feelings. 
Same thing with my mom. My mom used to talk with me touching my arm. Oh my goodness, just to remember that make me feel uneasy. 
When she does that I can't even understand what she is saying, my brain stops to process any input from my ears. 
But I can't ask her to stop to do that. I hate when I hurt someone's feelings so I prefer to suffer from these things to take a chance of hurting people, especially those who are special to me. 

I also have these troubles with my husband. Even though I love his touch and it makes me feel so good, there are sometimes that I can't handle it. 
In these times I recoil under his touch. I am a very very lucky girl because he is so understanding, he knows me and sees that when I am like this I need to be left alone for a while and he does that without thinking that he did something wrong. And after some time I go to him and hug him and say: "It's ok you can touch me now". One day he even asked me: "I'm going to hug you now, is that ok?". I can't express how much it meant to me! I could prepare myself for the touch, it made all the difference. Also, it meant to me that he really understands and respects my needs. Nobody has the right to always touch you, even if this person is really close because your body is yours only.

I hate when people from work touch me. Sometimes someone touches me and I want to lay down in fetal position crying because of the sensation the person leaves on my skin! In these situations, I would go to my husband (I'm lucky he works at the same building as I) and ask him to hold me tight at the same place the touch happened in an attempt to make the bad sensation go away.  He helps me to train to say to people don't touch me, but I haven't managed yet to express myself in this sense. At least, not at the touching moment... 

I used to have a colleague in work that he will forever be my reference on how I wish people treat me. 
He was absolutely friendly and nice and he never ever touched me, not even once! I love him for that haha. 
He is the living proof that you can be friendly without physical contact. I love that. 

I am training my mind to just say to people to not touch me, but I must confess it is still very hard to me.
